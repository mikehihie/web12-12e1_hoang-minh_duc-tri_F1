Flags & Safety

Green: Track Clear / Race Start
Yellow: Hazard ahead. Single: Slow down. Double: Be prepared to stop.
Red: Session Stopped (Bad weather/Crash).
Blue: Let faster car overtake (you are being lapped).
Black: Disqualified. Return to pits.
Chequered: End of race/session.

The 2025 F1 Car

F1 cars are the most technologically advanced racing machines on earth. The 2025 cars are the final evolution of the current "ground effect" era before the massive 2026 rule changes.

Technical Specs

  • Power Unit: 1.6L V6 Turbo Hybrid (~1000 HP)
  • Weight: Minimum ~798kg (including driver)
  • Top Speed: ~360 km/h (223 mph)
  • 0-100 km/h: ~2.5 seconds
  • G-Force: Up to 6G in corners
  • Fuel: E10 Sustainable Fuel Blend

Brief History

Formula 1 began in 1950 at Silverstone, UK. It has evolved through several iconic eras:

  • 1950s: The birth of the championship. Dominance by Fangio and Alfa Romeo/Mercedes.
  • 1960s: The "Garagistes" and the move to mid-engine cars. Jim Clark & Lotus.
  • 1970s: Aerodynamics and wings appear. The dangerous rivalry of Lauda vs Hunt.
  • 1980s: The Turbo Era. Massive horsepower (1000HP+) and the Senna vs Prost rivalry.
  • 1990s: Electronic aids (active suspension) and Michael Schumacher's rise.
  • 2000s: V10 screaming engines and Ferrari/Schumacher dominance.
  • 2014-Present: The Hybrid Era. Mercedes dominance followed by the Verstappen/Red Bull era.
